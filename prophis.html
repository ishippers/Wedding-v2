The Proposal

His Story


One of the weirdest things about proposing to someone is remembering that they have no idea what you are planning. You second guess yourself a lot and think on more than one occasion that the passing comment you just made would give the game away. For those of you that don’t know, Megan has wanted to be a spy ever since she was young. So of course she is rather perceptive at picking up on things. From the beginning I knew that the proposal would be one of the hardest things I would ever have to plan and pull off.


Sunday, October 27, may have been the day that it all came together but I had made my mind up about 8 months earlier. In February 2013 I told my parents that I wanted to make Megan my wife and that I was saving for a ring. It was a few months later after my mother had been to see my Grandmother in Australia that she took me too one side and showed me a beautiful ring which she believed belonged to her grandmother. When I saw the ring I knew it was exactly the kind of thing that Megan would love. I took it to the family jeweller and got it restored and polished. It was here that I found out that the ring was most likely made in around 1840 and therefore probably not my Great Grandmothers, but my Great Great Great Great Great Grandmothers.


So I had the ring that Megan deserved, now I just needed to ask her in an equally fitting way. I knew that there we lots of different places in London which had a special meaning to us. But none had as much as the OXO tower and the SouthBank where I had "looked after" Megan in August 2011, as a favour to her older sister.


Never being content with doing something simple I decided that the proposal should involve not only some of the places in London that mean a lot to us but also some of the people as well. So I started to call some of our closest friends and asked them to meet up with me on quiet Sunday afternoon.


Getting people to do meet you, in a specific place at a specific time, with out being able to give them good reason is really hard. In fact a few of them (they know who they are) were trying to bail or being really bad at committing so I had to send in Chris, the best man, to do a little bit of wrangling. Chris stepped up and having spent just over 6 months planning it looked like everything was going to come together nicely.
